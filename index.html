About Pythagorean Triples

A Pythagorean triple consists of three positive integers a, b, and c, such that a¬≤ + b¬≤ = c¬≤. The smallest and most famous example is (3, 4, 5).

Types of Triples:

  • Primitive: Triples where a, b, and c are coprime (e.g., 3, 4, 5)
  • Non-primitive: Multiples of primitive triples (e.g., 6, 8, 10)
